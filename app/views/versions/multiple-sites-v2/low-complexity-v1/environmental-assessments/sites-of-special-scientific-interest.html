How have you assessed the impact of your project on sites of special scientific interest (SSSI) and how you'll reduce this impact?

A SSSI is an area of land or coast that's legally protected because it's important for wildlife, plants, geological features, or natural habitats. SSSIs are managed by Natural England.

Read the guidance on SSSI (opens in new tab) to find out how to check if your project is within an SSSI.

If your project is in an SSSI you need to explain any impacts and what you'll do to reduce them.

For example, the impact of driving a truck through an SSSI area to do marine work is that it could crush rare plants or damage saltmarsh habitats the SSSI protects. To reduce this impact alternative routes have been considered and all vehicles will be kept on existing paved areas.

If there's no impact

If you do not think this applicable to your project and there is no impact you need to explain the reasons why.

For example, the work is 2km from the nearest SSSI so there is no impact to habitats or wildlife.
