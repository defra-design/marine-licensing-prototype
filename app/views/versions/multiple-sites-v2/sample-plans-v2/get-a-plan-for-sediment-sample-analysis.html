Based on your answers you need to apply for a marine licence to do dredging or disposal activities.

Before you apply for a marine licence

Send us some information to get a sample plan agreed

A sample plan lists the samples you need to take and how you need to take them. A sample plan is required by the London Convention and OSPAR convention to make sure any material being dredged or disposed is safe to be disposed of at sea.

You'll also need to agree to an estimate of costs for Â£3102.24 for the sampling before you can take samples.

Take samples

You need to collect the samples listed in your sample plan.

Get the samples analysed

The samples need to be analysed by a Marine Management Organisation (MMO) approved laboratory.

Costs for getting samples analysed are separate and not included as part of the estimate.

Apply for a marine licence

Once the samples have been analysed you need to send us the results as part of your application to get a marine licence.

There's a separate cost for a marine licence. The cost of a marine licence varies depending on what the licence is for.

If you apply for a marine licence without the required sample analysis the application will be put on hold until this is completed.
