Water Framework Directive

Before you start

The Water Framework Directive (WFD) protects the quality of estuarine and coastal waters. Its aim is to make sure all water bodies reach or maintain 'good' status.

We'll ask you some questions to find out whether you need to include a WFD assessment with your application. We'll ask you:

  • if your project is within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the coast
  • if your activity is one of the types that does not require an assessment
  • if you have a WFD assessment from a previous project and if anything has changed since it was done

Read the guidance Water Framework Directive (opens in new tab).

Excluded activities

Even if your project is within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the coast, some specific activities are excluded from having to provide a WFD assessment.

Previous WFD assessment

You may not need to complete a new WFD assessment if you carried out the same activity between 2015 and 2022 and already have an assessment.

You will still need a new assessment if:

  • you have changed how you carry out the activity - including the method, size, scale, volume, depth, location or timings
  • there has been a pollution incident in the area since you last carried out the activity

Uploading your assessment

You will need to upload a WFD assessment as part of your application if:

  • your project is within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the coast
  • your project is not limted to an excluded activity
  • you have a previous assessment from an activity carried out between 2015 to 2022 (you will still need to upload a new assessment if you do not have access to the previous one, you have changed how you carry out the activity, or there has been a pollution incident)
