Water Framework Directive

Before you start

The Water Framework Directive (WFD) protects the quality of estuarine and coastal waters. Its aim is to make sure all water bodies reach or maintain 'good' status.

We'll ask you some questions to find out whether you need to provide a WFD assessment with your application. We'll ask you if:

  • your project is within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the low water line, or in a tidal river or estuary - including the shore between low and high tide
  • your activity is one of the types that does not require an assessment

Read the guidance Water Framework Directive (opens in new tab).

Excluded activities

Even if your project is within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the low water line, or in a tidal river or estuary - including the shore between low and high tide, some specific activities are excluded from having to provide a WFD assessment.

Previous WFD assessments

You can provide a previous WFD assessment for the same activity in the same location, but it must be up to date.

You will need to carry out a new or updated assessment if:

  • the activity has changed in a way that could affect its impact on the water body
  • the status or objectives of the water body have changed since the assessment was completed
  • there is a new or increased risk that the water body will not meet its objectives
  • the previous assessment does not contain enough detail to support your current application
  • the assessment relates to an outdated river basin management plan cycle

Providing a WFD assessment

You will need to upload a WFD assessment as part of your application if:

  • your project is within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the low water line, or in a tidal river or estuary - including the shore between low and high tide
  • your project is not limited to an excluded activity
