Help with understanding the Water Framework Directive assessment area

The Water Framework Directive assessment area is anywhere in the marine environment from the Normal Tidal Limit (the furthest point upstream that the tide reaches) to within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the low water line (the edge of the sea at low tide).

This includes:

  • tidal rivers and estuaries
  • enclosed tidal waters, such as docks and marinas, where seawater can flow in and out, for example, through a lock
  • the shore between low tide and Mean High Water Springs (MHWS) - the average level the sea reaches at its highest tides
  • coastal areas within one nautical mile (1.85km) of the low-water line
