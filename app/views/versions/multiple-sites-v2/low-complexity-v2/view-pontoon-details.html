{# ============================== #} {# PROJECT DETAILS #} {# ============================== #}

Project details

Project background
Jurassic Coast SUP Ltd is a small paddleboarding hire and instruction business operating in Lyme Regis. We are applying for a marine licence to install a small floating pontoon within Lyme Regis Harbour to provide a safe and accessible launching and landing platform for paddleboards and kayaks hired to our customers. At present customers are required to enter the water via the main harbour slipway which is shared with commercial fishing vessels and other harbour traffic. During the summer months this creates significant health and safety concerns particularly for inexperienced users and those with limited mobility. The pontoon would be attached to the existing harbour wall using H-frame wall guides and a short hinged gangway. It will be a floating structure rising and falling with the tide. The works will take place entirely within the outer harbour area.
Preferred start and end dates of the licence
April 2026 to September 2027

{# ============================== #} {# SITE 1 — ACTIVITY 1 #} {# ============================== #}

Site 1 – Activity 1

Type of activity
Construction of new works
What you're constructing
Pontoons or floating walkways
Activity description
Installation of one floating pontoon unit measuring 8 metres in length by 4 metres in width (total area 32 m²). The pontoon will be manufactured off-site from HDPE modular float units set within a hot-dip galvanised steel frame with non-slip GRP mesh decking panels. The pontoon will be fixed to the existing harbour wall using two H-frame steel wall guide rails drilled and resin-anchored to the masonry. No piling or seabed penetration is required. Access to the pontoon from the quayside will be via a 4m hinged aluminium gangway. All works will be carried out from the quayside or using a small harbour workboat. No heavy plant or craneage is anticipated, individual pontoon components can be handled manually or with light lifting equipment.
Maximum duration of activity
6 weeks
Completion date
Not needed to be completed by a certain date
Activity limited to specific months
Yes — installation works to be carried out between April and September only, avoiding the winter months when harbour conditions make marine works impracticable and harbour traffic is at its lowest.
Proposed working hours
Monday to Friday, 08:00 to 17:00. Occasional Saturday morning working may be required to take advantage of suitable tidal windows.

{# ================================================= #} {# MARINE PLAN POLICIES — ENVIRONMENTAL #} {# ================================================= #}

Marine plan policies – Environmental

South West Air quality 1 (SW-AIR-1)
The project will have no impact on air quality. A small amount of machinery may be used during installation but any impact will be negligible, short term and localised.
South West Biodiversity 1 (SW-BIO-1)
The project is not anticipated to have any adverse impact on biodiversity. Lyme Regis Harbour is an existing built structure and the proposed pontoon location is within the main harbour basin which is regularly disturbed by vessel activity. A basic ecological assessment of the installation area has been carried out and no significant biodiversity features were identified. The underside of the pontoon may over time provide some habitat for colonising invertebrates and algae which could provide a minor biodiversity benefit.
South West Biodiversity 2 (SW-BIO-2)
The project should have no adverse impact on native species. No piling or seabed penetration is involved so there will be no direct physical disturbance to the subtidal substrate.
South West Biodiversity 3 (SW-BIO-3)
The proposed project should have no adverse impact on coastal ecosystems or habitats. The structure is small scale, reversible and does not require any modification to the existing coastline.
South West Disturbance 1 (SW-DIST-1)
The installation works are short in duration (estimated 3–4 days on the water). No impact piling is involved — all fixings are to the existing harbour wall. Any underwater noise from drilling activities will be brief and localised. Lyme Regis Harbour is an active harbour with regular vessel movements. The works will not materially increase disturbance beyond existing background levels. We do not consider a marine mammal observer to be necessary given the nature and scale of the works but we will comply with any requirements set by the MMO.
South West Habitats 1 (SW-HAB-1)
This project will have no impact on deep sea habitats. The works are within a small enclosed harbour basin in the intertidal/shallow subtidal zone.
South West Invasive non-native species 1 (SW-INNS-1)
All pontoon components and installation materials will be new and sourced from UK suppliers. There is therefore no risk of introducing invasive non-native species (INNS) from other waterbodies via the structure itself. Installation will be carried out by a local contractor whose equipment will not have been used in other waterbodies prior to works at Lyme Regis.
South West Invasive non-native species 2 (SW-INNS-2)
Once in place the pontoon will be used exclusively within Lyme Regis Harbour. All hired paddleboards and kayaks will be rinsed with fresh water and inspected between sessions as standard operating procedure for our business. We will display Check Clean Dry signage at the pontoon.
South West Marine litter 1 (SW-ML-1)
The installation works will not generate any marine litter. Any packaging or waste materials generated will be removed from site and disposed of appropriately. We will provide a small waste bin on the pontoon for customers to use and will conduct daily checks.
South West Marine litter 2 (SW-ML-2)
The proposed project does not involve any activities that are likely to increase marine litter in the area. Our business promotes responsible use of the marine environment and we actively encourage customers to take all litter away with them.
South West Marine protected areas 1 (SW-MPA-1)
The project site is within Lyme Regis Harbour. The Chesil Beach and Stennis Ledges SAC and West Bay SAC are located to the east and west respectively, at distances of several kilometres. The installation of a small floating pontoon within the existing harbour basin is not considered to have any adverse effect on these or other marine protected areas.
South West Marine protected areas 2 (SW-MPA-2)
The project will not have any impact on the ability of any MPA to adapt to climate change. The works are reversible and entirely within an existing built harbour structure.
South West Marine protected areas 3 (SW-MPA-3)
No impact on any Marine Conservation Zone features is anticipated. The nearest MCZ is the Lyme Bay and Torbay MCZ, the protected features of which are not present within the inner harbour area.
South West Underwater noise 1 (SW-UWN-1)
No impact piling is proposed. The only potential source of underwater noise is from the use of an SDS drill to install fixing bolts into the harbour wall. This will be intermittent, of very short duration and is not considered sufficient to cause any disturbance to marine mammals or other mobile species.
South West Underwater noise 2 (SW-UWN-2)
No significant underwater noise is anticipated from this project. The harbour is already regularly exposed to vessel noise and other anthropogenic noise sources.
South West Water quality 1 (SW-WQ-1)
No materials will be introduced to the water column that could cause deterioration in water quality. All materials to be used are inert (HDPE floats, aluminium, galvanised steel, GRP). Any construction activities that involve working over water will use appropriate containment measures to prevent the accidental release of any materials.
South West Water quality 2 (SW-WQ-2)
The project will have no impact on water quality. Lyme Regis Harbour is already classified under WFD and the installation of a small floating pontoon is not considered to have any bearing on the water body classification.
{# ================================================= #} {# MARINE PLAN POLICIES — SOCIAL #} {# ================================================= #}

Marine plan policies – Social

South West Access 1 (SW-ACC-1)
The proposed pontoon will improve public access to the water within Lyme Regis Harbour by providing a safe, stable and accessible boarding platform for paddleboards and kayaks hired to members of the public. The current access via the shared slipway is steep and can be slippery and access is often restricted when fishing vessels are using the same area. The pontoon will include a level walkway from the gangway which will make access significantly easier for people of all abilities including those with limited mobility. We will display clear and appropriate signage.
South West Employment 1 (SW-EMP-1)
Jurassic Coast SUP Ltd currently employs two full-time members of staff and two seasonal staff during the summer months. The installation of the pontoon will improve our operational efficiency and safety which we expect will allow us to increase throughput of customers and generate additional revenue. This will support the case for increasing staffing in future seasons. The project will also provide short-term employment for the local contractor carrying out the installation works.
South West Fisheries 1 (SW-FISH-1)
The pontoon will be positioned in the outer harbour away from the main fishing quay and slipway. We do not anticipate any negative impact on commercial fishing operations. We have discussed the proposed location with the harbour master and local fishing vessel operators and no objections were raised. The pontoon may in fact reduce conflicts between recreational users and fishing vessels at the shared slipway during busy summer periods.
South West Fisheries 2 (SW-FISH-2)
No impact on fisheries management or fish passage is anticipated. The pontoon is a floating structure with no seabed penetration and a minimal footprint of 24m². It will not obstruct any navigation channels or access routes used by fishing vessels.
South West Fisheries 3 (SW-FISH-3)
The project area is not within or adjacent to any known fish nursery areas, spawning grounds or migratory routes. Installation works are of short duration and low impact.
South West Heritage assets 1 (SW-HER-1)
Lyme Regis Harbour is a historic harbour but the proposed installation location is within the outer harbour basin rather than adjacent to any listed structures. We have checked the Historic Environment Record and are not aware of any heritage assets within the project area. The harbour authority has confirmed they have no heritage concerns regarding the proposed location.
South West Knowledge, understanding, appreciation and enjoyment 1 (SW-SOC-1)
Our business is entirely focused on improving public access to and enjoyment of the marine environment. We provide paddleboard lessons for beginners and run family hire sessions throughout the summer season. We actively promote awareness of the marine environment and coastal wildlife among our customers and include a brief environmental induction as part of all our sessions. The improved access that the pontoon will provide will allow us to extend our services to users who currently cannot access the water safely via the slipway, including older adults and families with young children.
South West Seascape and landscape 1 (SW-SCP-1)
The pontoon will be a small, low-profile floating structure that sits at water level and will be largely unobtrusive within the existing harbour environment. Lyme Regis Harbour already contains various pontoons, buoys, moorings and vessels. We do not believe the addition of this structure will have any adverse impact on the character or visual amenity of the harbour or the wider seascape. The structure will be finished in a neutral colour in keeping with harbour authority guidance.
South West Tourism and recreation 1 (SW-TR-1)
This project directly supports and enhances tourism and recreational activity at Lyme Regis. Our paddleboard hire and instruction business serves both local residents and the significant number of tourists who visit Lyme Regis and the Jurassic Coast each year. The improved access facilities will allow us to serve more customers safely and extend the season during which we can operate. Lyme Regis is a significant tourist destination and marine recreation is an important part of the visitor economy. The project is fully consistent with supporting this sector.
South West Tourism and recreation 2 (SW-TR-2)
The pontoon will improve the safety and ease of access for recreational water users. By providing a dedicated boarding point away from the main slipway we will also help reduce any potential conflicts between recreational paddlesport users and other harbour users including visiting leisure boats and fishing vessels.
